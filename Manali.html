Manali: An escape to life!


Manali is a must-visit destination for anyone seeking a unique and unforgettable travel experience. With its stunning natural beauty, thrilling adventures, rich culture, and warm hospitality, this charming hill station is sure to captivate your heart and leave you with lasting memories. Nestled in the Beas River Valley and is surrounded by snow-capped peaks, lush green forests, and gurgling streams. It is an ideal destination for nature lovers, adventure enthusiasts, and anyone seeking a break from the hustle and bustle of city life. The town is also known for its vibrant culture and traditions, which are reflected in its colourful festivals and events. In addition to its natural beauty and adventure activities, Manali is also known for its delicious food and warm hospitality. The town has a vibrant culinary scene that offers a range of local and international cuisines, including traditional Himachali dishes, Tibetan food, and popular street food.

  • Top 10 Places to Visit in Manali:


  1. Hidimba Temple:

    2. Also known as Dhungri Temple, the Hadimba Temple brings the epic, Mahabharata to life. Dedicated to Hidimba Devi, one of the wives of the Pandava Prince Bhima, the temple is nestled among tall deodar trees. The temple is built in pagoda style and its sanctum is a cave shrine believed to house the actual footprints of the deity. The temple without any doubt is one of the best places to visit in Manali with the snow-clad Himalayas in the backdrop.

  2. Solang Valley:

    3. An open valley of meadows, cobalt blue skies and spectacular vistas is the most popular tourist attraction in Manali. Solang Valley is a great melange of nature surrounded by the heavenly ranges of Himalayas.

  3. Rohtang Pass:

    4. On a winding mountain road, located on the Pir Panjal Range is the most touted tourist attraction of Manali, Rohtang Pass. A personal favourite of all nature lovers, artists and photographers the pass offers access to Lahaul and Spiti in summers.

  4. Bhrigu Lake:

    5. A gift of the Himalayas, Bhrigu Lake is located at the base of Bhrigu peak. It is one of the best trekking trails of Manali. The lake is surrounded by snow-clad peaks on all sides like in an attempt to protect it from the world.

  5. Jagatsukh:

    6. Jagatsukh is a fascinating settlement hidden in the mountains and famous for its temples. The backdrop of sparkling snow-capped peaks and deep green forests make visiting this place a visual pleasure. Jagatsukh is one of the main cities in the Kullu region and a good place to enjoy vernacular architecture. Those who want to hike through the Deo Tibba mountains can use Jagatsukh as a base. The Devi Sharbali temple located here is one of the many interesting places in the Manali region.

  6. Rahala Falls:

    7. During the monsoon and post-monsoon seasons, the Rahala waterfalls are an incredibly powerful sight, with its water rushing over the rocks and splashing visitors with fresh and cold mountain water. There are many picnic areas near the falls where you can enjoy the baskets and have fun with family and friends. There are many beautiful paths around the falls, and exploring the area through densely populated birch and silver birch is an experience in itself.

  7. Gluttony:

    8. A trip to Manali must include visits to nearby cities to experience the distinctive culture of the place. You won’t be disappointed if you add Gulaba to your list. A favorite place for winter travelers looking for snowy landscapes, the village has some of the most fascinating trails in Himachal Pradesh. Many consider it a good place to camp and observe the stars due to low light pollution and high altitude.

  8. Great Himalaya National Park:

    9. Considered one of the best places to visit in Manali and the surrounding regions, the Grand Himalayan National Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The park is home to many rare species, including over 209 bird species, 31 mammal species, and 1,000 plants. The receding snow leopard is the main predator here, and there are Himalayan tahr populations and brown bears. Photographers will love capturing the wildflowers and glacial valleys that dot the entire terrain. You can also participate in some fun naturalistic activities, such as hiking and camping in designated areas of the national park. Permits to enter the park must be obtained in advance and the park offers many simple but decent accommodation options.

  9. Arjun Gufa (Arjun cave):

    10. Located near the dizzying currents of the Beas River, the Arjun cave is associated with a legendary history of the Mahabharata Hindu epic. According to legend, Arjun, who was one of the Pandava brothers, meditated here. Arjun cave, one of the best places near Manali for a picnic, offers a breathtaking view of the surrounding hills and valleys. You can also visit Kunt Bhayo lake and Kunti Mata temple, two of the main attractions near the cave.

  10. Van Vihar:

    11. If you are looking for moments of tranquility in the bohemian surroundings of the city of Manali, a short walk in Van Vihar Park is recommended. One of Manali’s most popular attractions, Van Vihar is a public park with lush greenery and trees that touch the sky. You can also hop on a boat and maneuver around the reservoir here. Bird watchers will appreciate observing local species in the park, especially in the morning.
